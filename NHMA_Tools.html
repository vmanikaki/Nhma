Περιγραφή:
• This spindle is an excellent example of the fusion of artistic creation and religious expression, giving the traditional weaving tool a spiritual dimension. It is decorated with Byzantine-style icons, depicting religious figures such as Christ and archangels, imbuing the object with sanctity and symbolism. The spindle, normally intended for winding thread, here acquires a distinctive spiritual character, making it not only a production tool but also a symbol of faith and tradition. The icons on the wooden surfaces are meticulously painted with vivid colors and characteristic halos, elements reminiscent of ecclesiastical art, incorporating the centuries-old tradition of Byzantine iconography. The wooden parts of the spindle also feature carvings on the corners, which enhance the traditional aesthetic and reflect the care the craftsmen put into its construction. Its sturdy, triangular base ensures that each side of the spindle can be displayed individually, allowing the viewer to fully appreciate its artistic and spiritual value. The Penelope Gandhi Foundation, which collects and showcases such traditional tools, aims to preserve the heritage of weaving and highlight its connection with spirituality. Through this spindle, the artistic value, symbolic significance, and social role of the weaving craft are honored, making this spindle a symbol of both the cultural and religious heritage of Crete.• Αυτή η ανέμη αποτελεί ένα εξαιρετικό παράδειγμα της σύζευξης καλλιτεχνικής δημιουργίας και θρησκευτικής έκφρασης, αποδίδοντας στο παραδοσιακό εργαλείο της υφαντικής μια πνευματική διάσταση. Η ανέμη, που προορίζεται κανονικά για το τύλιγμα του νήματος, εδώ αποκτά έναν ξεχωριστό πνευματικό χαρακτήρα, κάνοντας την όχι μόνο εργαλείο παραγωγής αλλά και σύμβολο πίστης και παράδοσης. Οι αγιογραφίες στις ξύλινες επιφάνειες είναι λεπτομερώς ζωγραφισμένες, με ζωντανά χρώματα και χαρακτηριστικά φωτοστέφανα, στοιχεία που θυμίζουν εκκλησιαστική τέχνη και ενσωματώνουν τη μακραίωνη παράδοση των βυζαντινών εικόνων. Περιγραφή:
• The traditional Cretan loom displayed here is an exceptional example of the island’s rich weaving heritage and is part of the Penelope Gandhi Mission. Made from natural materials, primarily wood, the loom features a simple yet durable design, tailored for daily use. Particularly striking are the intricate carvings and decorative elements that adorn the loom, revealing the artisans' dedication to their craft and respect for the tool itself. The loom's wooden parts are decorated with detailed geometric patterns, flowers, and leaves, all hand-carved by skilled artisans. These engraved details serve not only aesthetic purposes but often carry symbolic significance. The motifs are chosen to provide protection to the weaver and ensure productive outcomes, while in some cases, they reflect family or local symbols, giving the loom a unique “identity” that unites generations and communities. This loom is not merely a tool for producing textiles but a valuable vessel of tradition and culture. It reflects the memory and values of Cretan communities, the self-sufficiency, and the social life of women’s communities, who used the art of weaving to create their daily fabrics. • The shuttles on the traditional Cretan loom, as seen in this image, are essential tools for the weaving process and are often crafted with great care. Cretan shuttles are usually wooden, long, and slender, with smooth surfaces to allow them to glide easily through the loom’s warp. In the center, there is a small groove or slot where the thread is placed, which unrolls as the shuttle passes from one end of the warp to the other. Despite their apparent simplicity, shuttles are an integral part of the weaving art and require skill to use.
